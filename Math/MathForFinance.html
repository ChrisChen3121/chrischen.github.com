1 Interest

$$A_0=Principal, r=rate, n=\#years, m=periods/year, k=\#periods$$

1.1 Simple Interest

$$V=(1+rn)A_0$$

1.2 Compound Interest

  • Interest is paid regularly $$V = (1+r)^nA_0$$
  • Interest rate r is yearly, interest is paid monthly $$V = [1+(r/m)]^kA_0$$

1.3 Present & Future Value

$$FV = (1+r)PV$$ $$PV = \frac{1}{1+r}FV = d_1FV$$

where \(d_1\) is the 1-year discount factor $$d_k = \frac{1}{[1+(r/m)]^k}$$

2 Annuities

An annuity is a contract that pays money regularly over a period of time. $$V=\frac{A}{r}[1-\frac{1}{(1+r)^n}]$$

2.1 Perpetual Annuity

A perpetual annuity pays a fixed amount periodically forever $$V=\frac{A}{r}$$
